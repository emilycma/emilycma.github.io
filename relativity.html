Stage Separation Bracket //

stage 2 vehicle structures

Terran R is a multistage rocket. Stage separation occurs once the vehicle reaches initial parking orbit. I was responsible for the initial design scoping of the stage 2 separation bracket, intended to fly with Terran R in 2026.

I cannot disclose design, methodology, or quantitative analysis to protect Relativity's intellectual property. Details have been kept purposely vague to avoid any unintentional disclosure of proprietary information. I collected requirements from every relevant system and presented to stakeholders. These requirements informed my intial design and sizing. Further sizing was completed with finite element software. This all culminated in a final review of my analysis. The results presented will influence many aspects of the critical design review for this component and relevant I defended my design decisions with FEM stress plots, bolt bush forces, lug and bolted joint margin calculations, I was the lead of these design meetings, and I was the responsible engineer for this component.

I am extremely thankful to my mentor for his guidance, knowledge, and support while giving me freedom to own my design and analysis decisions. I was uncertain with my placement on the structures team; my previous structures work was in undergrad, but most of my graduate program focused on materials analysis, manufacturing, and product realization. However, I enjoyed this internship immensely, and I learned a huge host of new software and analysis procedures in a short period of time. I am lucky to gain exposure to an aerospace company through work on such a high criticality component.
