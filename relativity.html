Stage Separation Bracket //

stage 2 vehicle structures, 2024

To protect Relativity's intellectual property, I cannot disclose design, methodology, or quantitative analysis. Details have been kept purposely vague to avoid any unintentional disclosure of proprietary information. Terran R is a multistage rocket. Stage separation occurs once the vehicle reaches initial parking orbit. I was responsible for the initial design scoping of the stage 2 separation bracket, intended to fly with Terran R in 2026. I collected requirements from every relevant system and presented to stakeholders. These requirements informed my intial design and sizing. Further sizing was completed with finite element software. This all culminated in a final review of my analysis. The results presented will influence many aspects of the critical design review for this component and relevant I defended my design decisions with FEM stress plots, bolt bush forces, lug and bolted joint margin calculations, I was the lead of these design meetings, and I was the responsible engineer for this component.

I am extremely thankful to my mentor for his guidance, knowledge, and support while giving me freedom to own my design and analysis decisions. I was uncertain with my placement on the structures team; most of my graduate program focused on materials analysis and manufacturing. However, I enjoyed this internship immensely, and I learned a huge host of new software and analysis procedures in a short period of time. I am lucky to gain exposure to an aerospace company through work on such a high criticality component.
