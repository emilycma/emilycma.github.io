name pendant //

马晴

This was my first experience with investment casting was through Stanford's Product Realization Lab. The part was modeled in SolidWorks, printed with castable wax resin with a Formlabs Form 3+. The sanded print is sprued and cast in fine plaster. The wax pattern is cleanly melted out of the flask. The hot flask is then placed into the casting machine, which melts the silver in a small crucible. The machine then pulls vacuum to draw silver into the plaster cavity.

This pendant shows my Chinese name.

Fabrication methods:

manual milling, sand casting, laser cutting, vinyl cutting, bead blasting
